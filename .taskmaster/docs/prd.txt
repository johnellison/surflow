<rpg-method>
# Surflow — Product Requirements Document (RPG Method)

Cross-platform surf lifestyle app combining real-time forecasts, webcams, guided physical therapy, and a local coach marketplace. Built for intermediate-to-advanced surfers in Morocco and Bali.
</rpg-method>

---

<overview>

## Problem Statement

Intermediate and advanced surfers juggle multiple apps and fragmented information to plan sessions, check conditions, recover from surf-related injuries, and find coaching. A surfer in Taghazout checks MSW for forecasts, opens a separate webcam app, searches YouTube for shoulder rehab exercises, and messages coaches on Instagram. There's no unified experience that ties the surf session lifecycle together: forecast → session → recovery → improvement.

Existing solutions fall short:
- **Surfline / Magic Seaweed**: Great forecasts, but no recovery or coaching. Webcams are paywalled or geo-limited.
- **PT apps (e.g., PLIABILITY)**: Generic. Not surf-specific. Don't connect to session context.
- **Coach discovery**: Entirely word-of-mouth or Instagram DMs. No booking, reviews, or quality signal.

Surflow closes this loop for committed surfers who treat surfing as a practice — not just a weekend hobby.

## Target Users

### Primary Persona: "The Committed Surfer"
- **Age**: 25–45
- **Level**: Intermediate to advanced (3+ years surfing)
- **Behavior**: Surfs 3–6x/week when conditions allow. Travels for surf (Morocco, Bali, Portugal, etc.)
- **Pain points**: Shoulder/hip/lower back issues from repetitive paddling. Wants structured improvement but can't find reliable coaches in transient surf destinations. Checks 3+ apps before every session.
- **Goals**: Surf more, recover faster, improve technique, find trusted local knowledge.

### Secondary Persona: "The Surf Coach"
- **Age**: 28–50
- **Level**: Advanced/professional, often certified (ISA, local federation)
- **Behavior**: Teaches 5–15 sessions/week. Currently finds students via hostel partnerships, Instagram, word-of-mouth.
- **Pain points**: Inconsistent bookings, no online presence beyond social media, no way to build a verified reputation.
- **Goals**: Fill schedule, build reviews, reach traveling surfers before they arrive.

## Success Metrics
- **Activation**: 60% of new users check a forecast + one other feature (webcam, PT, or coach) within first 3 days
- **Retention**: 40% weekly active users at day 30
- **Coach marketplace**: 20 coaches onboarded in Morocco + Bali within 3 months of launch
- **Session-to-recovery**: 25% of users who log a surf session start a PT routine within 24 hours
- **NPS**: >50 among weekly active users by month 6

## Capability Tree

### Capability: Surf Forecast Engine
Aggregates and normalizes swell, wind, tide, and weather data from multiple sources to present actionable surf conditions for specific spots.

#### Feature: Multi-Source Data Aggregation
- **Description**: Ingest forecast data from NOAA/NDBC buoys, Copernicus Marine Service, Open-Meteo, and Surfline (where API-accessible) into a normalized schema.
- **Inputs**: GPS coordinates of surf spots, time range (now + 7 days)
- **Outputs**: Normalized forecast object: swell height/period/direction, wind speed/direction, tide times/heights, water temp, air temp
- **Behavior**: Poll sources on schedule (hourly for swell, 3-hourly for wind). Merge and de-duplicate. Flag stale data. Prefer buoy data when available, fall back to model data.

#### Feature: Spot-Specific Forecast Display
- **Description**: Render forecast for a specific surf spot with a surf-quality rating (1–5 stars) and plain-language summary.
- **Inputs**: Normalized forecast data, spot metadata (orientation, reef/beach, ideal conditions)
- **Outputs**: Visual forecast card with star rating, swell/wind/tide summary, best-time-to-surf window
- **Behavior**: Apply spot-specific heuristics (e.g., "Anchor Point works best on NW swell 6-10ft, light E wind"). Generate natural-language summary ("Clean head-high sets, light offshores. Best around mid-tide at 10am.").

#### Feature: Spot Directory & Favorites
- **Description**: Searchable directory of surf spots in Morocco and Bali with metadata. Users can favorite spots for quick access.
- **Inputs**: User location or search query
- **Outputs**: List of spots with current conditions overview, distance, favorites toggle
- **Behavior**: Pre-loaded spot database (curated, not user-generated at launch). GPS-based sorting. Offline-capable spot metadata.

#### Feature: Multi-Day Forecast & Alerts
- **Description**: 7-day forecast view with push notification alerts when conditions match user preferences.
- **Inputs**: User alert preferences (min swell, max wind, preferred spots)
- **Outputs**: Push notifications, 7-day chart view
- **Behavior**: Evaluate alert rules against incoming forecast data. Deduplicate alerts (max 2/day per spot). Respect quiet hours.

---

### Capability: Webcam Viewer
Live and recent-replay webcam feeds for surf spots, enabling visual condition checks.

#### Feature: Live Webcam Streams
- **Description**: Display live webcam feeds from surf spots. Aggregate from public webcam providers and partner installations.
- **Inputs**: Spot ID
- **Outputs**: HLS/DASH video stream, still-image fallback
- **Behavior**: Detect stream availability. Show "offline" badge when unavailable. Prefer HD streams on Wi-Fi, downscale on cellular. Display timestamp overlay.

#### Feature: Rewind / Time-Lapse
- **Description**: View recent webcam footage (last 4 hours) as a time-lapse or scrubable replay.
- **Inputs**: Spot ID, time range
- **Outputs**: Time-lapse video, scrubber UI
- **Behavior**: Store snapshots every 5 minutes server-side. Assemble into playable time-lapse on demand. Cache aggressively.

#### Feature: Webcam + Forecast Overlay
- **Description**: Overlay current swell/wind/tide data on the webcam view for at-a-glance context.
- **Inputs**: Live webcam feed, current forecast data for the same spot
- **Outputs**: Composited view with data overlay
- **Behavior**: Semi-transparent overlay on bottom third of video. Tap to toggle. Shows star rating, swell, wind, tide.

---

### Capability: Physical Therapy & Recovery
Guided, surf-specific exercise routines for injury prevention and post-session recovery.

#### Feature: Exercise Library
- **Description**: Curated library of surf-specific PT exercises with video demonstrations, targeting shoulders, hips, lower back, ankles, and neck.
- **Inputs**: Body area filter, difficulty level
- **Outputs**: Exercise cards with video, description, duration, sets/reps, difficulty
- **Behavior**: Exercises tagged by body area, surf-relevance (paddling, pop-up, bottom turn), and difficulty. Video plays inline. Offline download available.

#### Feature: Guided Routines
- **Description**: Pre-built and algorithmically suggested routines (warm-up, cool-down, recovery day, injury-specific).
- **Inputs**: Routine type, user injury history (optional), session duration
- **Outputs**: Ordered exercise sequence with rest timers, progress indicator
- **Behavior**: Timer-based flow. Audio cues for transitions. Track completion. Suggest recovery routine after a surf session is logged.

#### Feature: Session-to-Recovery Link
- **Description**: After a user logs a surf session (or one is detected), prompt them with a relevant recovery routine.
- **Inputs**: Session log (duration, intensity, spot)
- **Outputs**: Push notification or in-app prompt with recommended routine
- **Behavior**: Trigger 1–4 hours post-session. Intensity-based recommendation (longer session → longer routine). Dismissable.

#### Feature: Progress Tracking
- **Description**: Track PT routine completion over time. Show streaks, consistency, and body-area coverage.
- **Inputs**: Completed routine logs
- **Outputs**: Calendar heatmap, streak counter, body-area balance chart
- **Behavior**: Local-first tracking. Sync to cloud for cross-device. Weekly summary notification.

---

### Capability: Coach Marketplace
Two-sided marketplace connecting traveling surfers with local coaches for lessons, video analysis, and guided sessions.

#### Feature: Coach Profiles
- **Description**: Public coach profiles with bio, certifications, specialties, photos/videos, pricing, and verified reviews.
- **Inputs**: Coach-submitted profile data, verification documents
- **Outputs**: Public profile page, search result card
- **Behavior**: Coaches self-manage profiles. Admin review for initial activation. Certification badges (ISA, etc.) verified manually at launch.

#### Feature: Search & Discovery
- **Description**: Search coaches by location, specialty (beginner lessons, advanced coaching, video analysis), availability, rating, and price.
- **Inputs**: User location or destination, filters
- **Outputs**: Ranked list of coaches with availability preview
- **Behavior**: Default sort by proximity + rating. Show "available this week" badge. Filter by language spoken.

#### Feature: Booking & Scheduling
- **Description**: Book a session with a coach. Coaches set availability windows and session types (1-on-1, group, video analysis).
- **Inputs**: Coach ID, session type, preferred date/time
- **Outputs**: Booking confirmation, calendar entry for both parties
- **Behavior**: Real-time availability check. Instant booking (no request/accept flow at launch). 24-hour free cancellation. Timezone-aware.

#### Feature: In-App Messaging
- **Description**: Direct messaging between surfer and coach, pre- and post-booking.
- **Inputs**: User IDs, message text, optional media attachments
- **Outputs**: Chat thread
- **Behavior**: Push notifications for new messages. Message history persisted. No phone number exchange required. Media support for sharing wave photos/video clips.

#### Feature: Payments
- **Description**: In-app payment processing for coach bookings with Surflow taking a platform fee.
- **Inputs**: Booking details, payment method (card, Apple Pay)
- **Outputs**: Payment confirmation, receipt, coach payout
- **Behavior**: Stripe Connect for split payments. Coach receives payout after session completion (24h hold). Refund on cancellation within policy. Platform fee: 15%.

#### Feature: Reviews & Ratings
- **Description**: Post-session review system. Surfers rate coaches; coaches can respond.
- **Inputs**: Completed booking, star rating (1–5), text review
- **Outputs**: Review on coach profile, aggregate rating update
- **Behavior**: Review prompt 2 hours after session end time. Coach can respond publicly. Flagging system for abuse. Minimum 3 reviews before aggregate shown.

---

### Capability: User Identity & Session Management
Authentication, profiles, and session logging.

#### Feature: Authentication
- **Description**: Sign up / sign in via email+password, Apple Sign-In, and Google Sign-In.
- **Inputs**: Credentials or OAuth token
- **Outputs**: Authenticated session, JWT
- **Behavior**: Apple Sign-In required for iOS App Store. Email verification. Password reset flow. Session refresh tokens.

#### Feature: User Profiles
- **Description**: User profile with name, photo, home break, surf level, injury history (optional), and preferences.
- **Inputs**: User-submitted data
- **Outputs**: Profile object, settings
- **Behavior**: Minimal required fields (name, email). Optional enrichment for better recommendations. Profile visible to coaches when booking.

#### Feature: Surf Session Logging
- **Description**: Log surf sessions with spot, duration, conditions, and personal notes.
- **Inputs**: Spot (auto-suggested from location), start/end time, conditions (auto-filled from forecast), notes, rating
- **Outputs**: Session log entry, feeds into PT recommendations and personal stats
- **Behavior**: Quick-log (one tap: "I surfed here for 2 hours") or detailed log. Auto-suggest spot based on GPS. Pull conditions from forecast data.

---

### Capability: Platform Infrastructure
Cross-cutting concerns: notifications, offline support, analytics.

#### Feature: Push Notifications
- **Description**: Unified notification system for forecast alerts, PT reminders, booking updates, and messages.
- **Inputs**: Notification trigger events
- **Outputs**: Push notification (APNs for iOS, web push for browser)
- **Behavior**: User-configurable per category. Quiet hours respected. Badge count management.

#### Feature: Offline Support
- **Description**: Core forecast data, spot metadata, and PT exercise videos available offline.
- **Inputs**: User's favorited spots, downloaded routines
- **Outputs**: Cached data accessible without network
- **Behavior**: Background sync when online. Stale data indicator. Offline-first for PT videos.

#### Feature: Analytics & Telemetry
- **Description**: Track user behavior for product decisions. Privacy-respecting, no PII in analytics.
- **Inputs**: User events (screen views, feature usage, session logs)
- **Outputs**: Analytics dashboard (Mixpanel or PostHog)
- **Behavior**: Consent-based. GDPR-compliant. Event schema defined upfront.

## Module Definitions

### Module: packages/core
- **Maps to capability**: All capabilities (shared business logic)
- **Responsibility**: Platform-agnostic business logic, data transformations, and types
- **Exports**:
  - `normalizeForecast()` — merge multi-source data into unified format
  - `rateSurfConditions()` — 1–5 star rating for a spot given conditions
  - `suggestRecoveryRoutine()` — recommend PT routine based on session
  - `searchCoaches()` — filter/sort coach results
  - All shared TypeScript types and Zod schemas

### Module: packages/ui
- **Maps to capability**: Presentation layer across all capabilities
- **Responsibility**: Shared React components that work on both mobile (React Native) and web
- **Exports**:
  - `<ForecastCard>`, `<SpotCard>`, `<ExerciseCard>`, `<CoachCard>`
  - `<StarRating>`, `<ConditionsBadge>`, `<TimerView>`
  - Primitive components: `<Button>`, `<Text>`, `<Card>`, `<Input>`

### Module: packages/api-client
- **Maps to capability**: Data layer for all capabilities
- **Responsibility**: Typed HTTP client for communicating with the backend API
- **Exports**:
  - `forecastApi.getSpotForecast(spotId)`, `forecastApi.getAlerts()`
  - `webcamApi.getStream(spotId)`, `webcamApi.getTimelapse(spotId, range)`
  - `ptApi.getExercises(filters)`, `ptApi.getRoutine(type)`
  - `coachApi.search(filters)`, `coachApi.book(coachId, details)`
  - `authApi.signIn()`, `authApi.signUp()`, `authApi.refresh()`

### Module: services/api
- **Maps to capability**: All capabilities (server-side)
- **Responsibility**: REST API, database access, third-party integrations, background jobs
- **Key sub-modules**:
  - `routes/` — HTTP endpoint handlers
  - `services/` — Business logic (decoupled from HTTP)
  - `db/` — Drizzle ORM schema, migrations, queries
  - `jobs/` — Cron-like background tasks (forecast polling, notification dispatch)
  - `integrations/` — NOAA, Copernicus, Stripe, APNs clients

### Module: apps/mobile
- **Maps to capability**: Mobile presentation for all capabilities
- **Responsibility**: Expo/React Native iOS app with tab navigation
- **Key screens**: Forecast tab, Webcams tab, Recovery tab, Coaches tab, Profile tab

### Module: apps/web
- **Maps to capability**: Web presentation for all capabilities + marketing
- **Responsibility**: Next.js web app for browser access + marketing/landing pages
- **Key routes**: `/forecast`, `/webcams`, `/recovery`, `/coaches`, `/profile`

## Dependency Chain

### Foundation Layer (Phase 0)
No dependencies — these are built first.

- **packages/core/types**: Shared TypeScript types and Zod schemas (Spot, Forecast, Exercise, Coach, User, Session, Booking)
- **services/api/db**: Database schema and migrations (Postgres via Drizzle). Tables for users, spots, exercises, coaches, bookings, sessions, reviews.
- **Monorepo scaffold**: Turborepo config, workspace setup, ESLint/Prettier, CI skeleton

### Data Layer (Phase 1)
- **services/api/auth**: Depends on [core/types, db]. JWT-based auth, Apple/Google OAuth, session management.
- **services/api/integrations/forecast-sources**: Depends on [core/types]. NOAA, Copernicus, Open-Meteo API clients.
- **packages/core/forecast**: Depends on [core/types]. Normalization, aggregation, and rating logic.
- **packages/core/spots**: Depends on [core/types]. Spot search, distance calculation, favorites.

### Core API Layer (Phase 2)
- **services/api/routes/auth**: Depends on [api/auth]. Sign up, sign in, refresh, profile endpoints.
- **services/api/routes/forecast**: Depends on [core/forecast, integrations/forecast-sources, db]. Forecast endpoints per spot.
- **services/api/routes/spots**: Depends on [core/spots, db]. Spot directory, search, favorites endpoints.
- **services/api/jobs/forecast-poller**: Depends on [integrations/forecast-sources, db]. Background job to poll and cache forecast data.
- **packages/api-client**: Depends on [core/types]. Typed HTTP client (can be built in parallel with API routes using types as contract).

### Content Layer (Phase 3)
- **services/api/routes/pt**: Depends on [db, auth]. Exercise and routine CRUD, progress tracking endpoints.
- **packages/core/pt**: Depends on [core/types]. Routine suggestion engine, session-to-recovery logic.
- **services/api/routes/webcams**: Depends on [db, auth]. Webcam metadata, stream URLs, snapshot storage.

### Marketplace Layer (Phase 4)
- **services/api/routes/coaches**: Depends on [db, auth, core/types]. Coach profiles, search, availability.
- **services/api/routes/bookings**: Depends on [coaches, auth, db]. Booking creation, Stripe Connect integration.
- **services/api/integrations/stripe**: Depends on [core/types]. Stripe Connect for split payments, refunds.
- **services/api/routes/reviews**: Depends on [bookings, auth, db]. Post-session reviews and ratings.
- **services/api/routes/messages**: Depends on [auth, db]. Real-time messaging between surfers and coaches.

### Presentation Layer (Phase 5)
- **packages/ui**: Depends on [core/types]. Shared UI component library.
- **apps/mobile**: Depends on [packages/ui, packages/api-client, core/types]. Full mobile app.
- **apps/web**: Depends on [packages/ui, packages/api-client, core/types]. Full web app.

### Polish Layer (Phase 6)
- **services/api/routes/notifications**: Depends on [auth, bookings, forecast-poller]. Push notification dispatch.
- **services/api/routes/sessions**: Depends on [auth, db, core/forecast, core/pt]. Session logging with auto-suggestions.
- **Offline support**: Depends on [api-client, core/forecast, core/pt]. Cache layer in mobile + web.
- **Analytics integration**: Depends on [apps/mobile, apps/web]. Event tracking setup.

## Test Pyramid

```
        /\
       /E2E\       ← 10% (Critical user journeys: forecast check, booking flow, PT routine)
      /------\
     /Integration\ ← 30% (API endpoints, database queries, third-party integrations)
    /------------\
   /  Unit Tests  \ ← 60% (Core logic: forecast normalization, rating engine, routine suggestions)
  /----------------\
```

## Coverage Requirements
- Line coverage: 80% minimum (packages/core)
- Branch coverage: 70% minimum
- Function coverage: 85% minimum
- Statement coverage: 80% minimum

## Critical Test Scenarios

### Forecast Engine
**Happy path**: Multi-source data merges into correct unified forecast with accurate rating
**Edge cases**: One source down (graceful degradation), stale data detection, spots with no buoy coverage
**Error cases**: All sources timeout, malformed API responses, rate limiting
**Integration**: Forecast poller → DB → API → client rendering pipeline

### Coach Marketplace
**Happy path**: Search → view profile → book → pay → message → complete → review
**Edge cases**: Concurrent booking for same slot, coach cancels, timezone edge cases
**Error cases**: Stripe payment failure, webhook retry, coach account deactivation mid-booking
**Integration**: Stripe Connect onboarding, payment split, refund flow

### Physical Therapy
**Happy path**: Complete guided routine with timers, log completion, see progress
**Edge cases**: App backgrounded mid-routine, offline routine completion
**Error cases**: Video fails to load, timer desync
**Integration**: Session log triggers recovery suggestion → correct routine selected

## Test Generation Guidelines
- Use Vitest for unit and integration tests
- Use Playwright for web E2E, Detox or Maestro for mobile E2E
- Mock third-party APIs (NOAA, Stripe) with recorded responses for CI
- Use Stripe test mode with test clock for payment lifecycle testing
- Test forecast rating engine with known-condition fixtures (e.g., "perfect day at Anchor Point" should rate 5 stars)

## Technology Stack

| Layer | Technology | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Mobile | Expo (React Native) | Code sharing with web, OTA updates, strong iOS support |
| Web | Next.js 16 (App Router) | SSR for marketing/SEO, app routes for authenticated experience |
| Shared UI | Tamagui or React Native Web | Cross-platform component primitives |
| API | Hono (on Node.js) | Lightweight, fast, TypeScript-native, Vercel Functions compatible |
| Database | Neon (Serverless Postgres) | Branching for dev, serverless scaling, Vercel Marketplace |
| Cache | Upstash Redis | Session cache, rate limiting, real-time pub/sub for messaging |
| File storage | Vercel Blob | Coach photos, exercise videos, webcam snapshots |
| Payments | Stripe Connect | Marketplace split payments, Apple Pay support |
| Auth | Supabase Auth or Clerk | Apple Sign-In, Google OAuth, JWT management |
| Push notifications | Expo Notifications (APNs) + web-push | Unified push across platforms |
| Monorepo | Turborepo | Build caching, workspace management |
| Analytics | PostHog | Privacy-friendly, self-hostable, feature flags |
| CI/CD | GitHub Actions + Vercel | Auto-deploy web, EAS Build for mobile |

**Decision: Expo (React Native) over native Swift**
- **Rationale**: Maximizes code sharing between iOS and web (packages/core, packages/ui). Single TypeScript codebase. Expo's managed workflow handles App Store submissions, OTA updates, and push notifications.
- **Trade-offs**: Slight performance hit vs native for video-heavy features (webcams). Mitigated by using native video player modules.
- **Alternatives considered**: SwiftUI (better perf, no code sharing), Flutter (Dart ecosystem doesn't align with Next.js web).

**Decision: Hono over tRPC for API**
- **Rationale**: Lightweight, works as Vercel Functions. REST is better for the marketplace (public coach profiles, SEO). Type safety via Zod schemas shared from packages/core.
- **Trade-offs**: No automatic type inference like tRPC. Mitigated by shared Zod schemas and typed api-client package.
- **Alternatives considered**: tRPC (tighter types, but poor fit for public API surface), Express (heavier, less TypeScript-native).

<risks>

## Technical Risks

**Risk**: Forecast data source APIs are unreliable or rate-limited
- **Impact**: High — core value proposition breaks
- **Likelihood**: Medium — NOAA is stable but slow; Copernicus has auth complexity
- **Mitigation**: Multi-source aggregation with graceful degradation. Aggressive caching (hourly refresh). Stale-data indicators in UI.
- **Fallback**: Pre-compute forecasts server-side on schedule rather than real-time fetching.

**Risk**: Webcam feed availability is inconsistent across regions
- **Impact**: Medium — feature works in some spots but not others
- **Likelihood**: High — Morocco has fewer public webcams than established surf regions
- **Mitigation**: Start with available feeds, mark spots without cams clearly. Explore partnerships with local surf camps for camera installations.
- **Fallback**: Crowd-sourced "conditions check-in" feature as alternative visual confirmation.

**Risk**: Cross-platform UI consistency between React Native and Next.js
- **Impact**: Medium — divergent UX between platforms
- **Likelihood**: Medium — Tamagui/RN Web bridging is maturing but has edge cases
- **Mitigation**: Shared design tokens and component library. Accept platform-appropriate differences. Test both platforms in CI.
- **Fallback**: Separate component implementations sharing only business logic.

## Dependency Risks

**Risk**: Stripe Connect onboarding friction for coaches in Morocco/Bali
- **Impact**: High — marketplace can't function without payments
- **Likelihood**: Medium — Stripe availability varies by country
- **Mitigation**: Research Stripe availability in Morocco and Indonesia early. Identify alternative payment processors if needed (Paystack for Morocco, Xendit for Indonesia).
- **Fallback**: Manual invoicing for initial coach cohort while payments are resolved.

**Risk**: Apple App Store review rejection
- **Impact**: High — blocks iOS launch
- **Likelihood**: Low-Medium — marketplace apps require careful compliance
- **Mitigation**: Follow Apple guidelines for marketplace apps (in-app purchase vs. physical service exemption). Apple Sign-In included from day one.
- **Fallback**: TestFlight distribution for initial user testing while resolving review issues.

## Scope Risks

**Risk**: Building too much before validating core value (forecast + recovery loop)
- **Impact**: High — wasted effort if core assumption is wrong
- **Likelihood**: Medium
- **Mitigation**: Phase 1 delivers forecast-only MVP. Validate with 50 surfers in Taghazout before building marketplace.
- **Fallback**: Pivot to forecast-only app if marketplace doesn't gain traction.

**Risk**: Exercise content creation bottleneck
- **Impact**: Medium — PT feature feels thin without enough exercises
- **Likelihood**: Medium — video production takes time
- **Mitigation**: Start with 20 exercises (minimum viable library). Partner with a surf PT professional for content. Text + illustration first, video second.
- **Fallback**: Link to curated external content while building own library.

</risks>

## Glossary
- **Spot**: A specific surf break location (e.g., "Anchor Point", "Uluwatu")
- **Swell**: Ocean wave energy traveling across the surface, measured in height (ft/m), period (seconds), and direction (degrees)
- **Offshore wind**: Wind blowing from land to sea, grooming wave faces — ideal conditions
- **Onshore wind**: Wind blowing from sea to land, making waves choppy — poor conditions
- **Lineup**: The area where surfers sit waiting for waves
- **Pop-up**: The motion of going from prone to standing on the board
- **PT**: Physical therapy
- **ISA**: International Surfing Association (coaching certification body)
